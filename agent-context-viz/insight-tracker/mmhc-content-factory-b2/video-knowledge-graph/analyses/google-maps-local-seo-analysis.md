## 1. CORE THESIS

If your business isn't in the top 3 results of Google Maps' "map pack," you're invisible. Those 3 spots get 70%+ of all phone calls. The rest fight for scraps. This isn't mysterious — Google tells you exactly what signals it needs: relevance, reputation, and proximity. The businesses that rank aren't lucky; they're doing the work.

**For MMHC:** This is patient acquisition 101. When someone in NYC searches "therapist near me" or "anxiety therapist Manhattan," the map pack determines whether they call you or a competitor. MMHC should be dominating this.

## 2. KEY ARGUMENTS & EVIDENCE

**Argument 1: The Map Pack Is the Entire Game**
- Top 3 Google Maps results get 70%+ of calls
- People don't scroll past the map pack for local services
- Visual trust (reviews, photos, hours) beats organic search results
- Evidence: Live demo searching "plumber New Orleans" — nobody clicks "more businesses"

**Argument 2: Three Reasons You're Not Ranking**
1. **Google doesn't understand your business** — Missing/poor service pages, unoptimized Google Business Profile
2. **Google doesn't trust your business** — Few reviews, inconsistent NAP (Name/Address/Phone) across directories, no backlinks
3. **You're not doing the work your competitors are** — They're actively optimizing while you're hoping to get noticed

**Argument 3: Three Signals Google Uses to Rank**
1. **Relevance** — Categories, services, service pages on website, keyword-rich content
2. **Reputation/Prominence** — Review count, quality, frequency + backlinks + citations
3. **Proximity** — Physical distance from searcher (hardest to control)

**Argument 4: The Fix Is Systematic, Not Magical**
- Case study: Plumbing company went from invisible to 100 leads/month in 5 months
- Case study: Landscaping company hit 286 leads/month, now turning away work
- All from fixing Google Business Profile + website + trust signals

## 6. CONTRADICTIONS / TENSIONS

- **Proximity signal conflicts with online therapy model.** MMHC serves all of NY State online, but Google Maps is proximity-biased. For "therapist near me" searches, a Manhattan office address helps. But for "online therapy New York," proximity matters less — content relevance dominates.

- **High-volume review systems in healthcare have compliance considerations.** The video targets plumbers/roofers where review solicitation is straightforward. For therapy practices, asking for reviews requires careful framing — can't reference treatment details, must protect confidentiality.
