When to consider couples counseling

You don’t have to be “at the breaking point” to get help. Many couples start when they notice a pattern they can’t shift on their own.

Common reasons couples reach out

  • Recurring arguments that escalate quickly or never get resolved
  • Feeling unheard, dismissed, or like you’re walking on eggshells
  • Rebuilding trust after a rupture or a long period of distance
  • Stress transitions: moving, parenting, career changes, finances
  • Different communication styles (pursue/withdraw, shutdown, defensiveness)

It can also help when things look “fine”

  • You want stronger repair skills for conflict
  • You’re making a major decision and want clearer teamwork
  • You want more emotional closeness and better day-to-day communication
  • You want a neutral space to talk without spiraling into blame

Getting started together

If it’s hard to even agree on therapy, start with one small agreement: we’ll try a few sessions and evaluate.

A simple first conversation

  • What do we want to be different in 3 months?
  • What keeps going wrong in the same moments?
  • What would help us feel safer during conflict?
  • How will we know therapy is helping?

What you can do today

  • Choose one recurring issue you want to work on first
  • Pick a weekly time you can protect (even if it’s not perfect)
  • Decide on one “pause” phrase to de-escalate arguments
  • Book an intake and let the therapist guide the structure

FAQ

Is online couples counseling effective?

Many couples find online counseling effective because it makes consistency easier and keeps sessions grounded in your day-to-day environment. The biggest drivers of progress are a good therapist fit, clear goals, and showing up regularly.

Do we need to live in NYC to start virtual couples therapy with MMHC?

No. MMHC is online-only and serves clients across New York State, including NYC. Generally, you’ll want to be physically located in New York for sessions.

What if my partner is unsure about therapy?

That’s common. Couples therapy isn’t about proving who’s right—it’s about changing the pattern. A therapist can help you both stay engaged, set shared goals, and make the work feel practical.

Can couples counseling help with recurring arguments?

Yes—often by focusing on the pattern rather than the topic. You’ll work on slowing escalation, listening more effectively, and building repair skills so conflict doesn’t linger for days.

Do you take insurance for online couples counseling?

MMHC works with most major insurance plans. Coverage can vary by plan and provider, so the team can help you understand your options and next steps.
