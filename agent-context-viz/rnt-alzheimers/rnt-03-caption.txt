One of the most common things we hear from clients is: "I thought I was being productive by thinking through every scenario."

But there's a difference between planning and looping. Planning has an endpoint. Looping doesn't. And research now shows that chronic repetitive negative thinking isn't just exhausting — it's associated with the physical accumulation of Alzheimer's-related proteins in the brain.

Your brain activates the same stress pathways whether the threat is real or imagined. When those pathways stay on for years, the consequences go beyond anxiety.

CBT, mindfulness, and targeted talk therapy can help you distinguish between useful concern and destructive rumination. That's not a platitude — it's what the science supports.
