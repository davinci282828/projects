A 2020 study from University College London found that people who engage in repetitive negative thinking — ruminating on the past, worrying obsessively about the future — had higher levels of tau and amyloid protein deposits in their brains. Those are the same proteins linked to Alzheimer's disease.

This isn't about having a bad day. It's about chronic patterns. The loops you can't break out of. The 2 AM replay of something that happened years ago.

The good news: thought patterns are modifiable. That's exactly what therapy is designed to do — interrupt the loop before it becomes the default.
