You know the one. You're lying in bed and your brain decides it's time to replay every mistake you've made this year. Or catastrophize about tomorrow. Or dissect a conversation from three weeks ago.

We've all been there. But when it's chronic — when the loop is the default, not the exception — it's more than a bad night. A landmark study found that repetitive negative thinking is associated with higher levels of dementia-related proteins in the brain and faster cognitive decline.

This isn't about "just thinking positive." It's about building the skills to catch the loop, step out of it, and choose a different response. That's learnable. That's what therapy teaches.
